Year

2020

Problem

Students have difficulty memorizing Kanji characters

As Japanese language learners, I see many of my friends have a difficulty memorizing Japanese kanji. They usually memorize them in a matter of weeks and then, despite their best efforts, forget them. The kanji their memorize are the ones they commonly use in dairy life.

Research

Spaced-repetition is the best way for memorization

While researching the best way to memorize kanji, I came across an article by Kanshudo on using the spaced-repetition approach, which involves memorizing the same kanji repeatedly when you first learn about them and less frequently as time goes on. Most schools already use this principle to help with memorization such as, giving kanji homework, using flashcards, and repeating kanji before starting class start, but many of them fail since it takes so much effort to keep up the practice.

Solution

Using minimal effort is a key

For the spaced-repetition memorization to work, the user must continue to use the product even after they have memorized the kanji, so I kept these criteria in mind when designing the Kanji memorization sticker.

1. The product must be able to remind the user of the Kanji on a daily basis.

2. The product should require little mental effort to use.

3. The product should be enjoyable, simple to use, and integrated into the user's daily life.

testing + Improvement

Users need image to quickly regonize them

After the first iteration of the stickers, I put them to the test with my friends. They agreed that the product is beneficial, but it takes some effort to read the definition. As a result, I include the image on the stickers so that users can identify them more quickly
